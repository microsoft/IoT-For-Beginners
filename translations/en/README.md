# IoT for Beginners - A Curriculum  

Azure Cloud Advocates at Microsoft are excited to present a 12-week, 24-lesson curriculum focused on the basics of IoT. Each lesson includes pre- and post-lesson quizzes, step-by-step instructions, solutions, assignments, and more. Our project-based approach ensures you learn by doing, which is a proven way to retain new skills.  

The projects follow the journey of food from farm to table, covering farming, logistics, manufacturing, retail, and consumer use‚Äîkey industries for IoT applications.

## Pedagogy  

We designed this curriculum with two key principles: it‚Äôs project-based and includes frequent quizzes. By the end of this series, students will have built a plant monitoring and watering system, a vehicle tracker, a smart factory setup to monitor and inspect food, and a voice-controlled cooking timer. They will also learn the fundamentals of IoT, including writing device code, connecting to the cloud, analyzing telemetry, and running AI on the edge.  

By aligning the content with projects, the learning process becomes more engaging, and students are more likely to retain the concepts.  

Additionally, low-stakes quizzes before a lesson help students focus on the topic, while post-lesson quizzes reinforce their understanding. This curriculum is designed to be flexible and enjoyable, and it can be completed in its entirety or in parts. The projects start simple and gradually become more complex over the 12-week program.  

Each project uses real-world hardware that is accessible to students and hobbyists. The projects also provide relevant background knowledge about the specific domain. Understanding the context of the problems they are solving helps students think critically about their IoT solutions and their real-world applications.

## Each lesson includes:  

- Sketchnote  
- Optional supplemental video

They are linked within the lessons, but the quiz app can also be run locally or deployed to Azure. Follow the instructions in the `quiz-app` folder. Localization of quizzes is ongoing.
